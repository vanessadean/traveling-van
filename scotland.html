Scotland


Scotland

I've been to Scotland a few times and this last trip was a quick one to Loch Lomond, near Glasgow, for a wedding. Scottish weddings weren't something on my radar before this trip, but now I know better than to pass up an opportunity to party with the Scots. Aside from the fact that the bride and groom were two of the loveliest people I've ever met, with the nicest friends and family, there was plenty of good food and booze, a band playing traditional Scottish music, and a little something called Ceilidh dancing. It felt a lot wilder in person, but here is a little taste.



I definitely lost a shoe during one dance and had my feet trod on, but it was totally worth it. The wedding and reception were held in a beautiful old baronial manor house turned youth hostel, where everyone was booked in for the weekend. The Loch Lomond Youth Hostel has since closed, which is a shame because it was a fantastic place to host a party.

After the wedding we spent just one night in Glasgow before heading back to the States. We stayed in a trendy-looking little hostel in the city center called The Brunswick. Our room was modern but cosy and the cafe downstairs opened up bright and early so we could get a bite to eat before heading to the airport.

The best surprise for me in Glasgow was the incredibly tasty food at Dakhin, a South Indian restaurant right around the corner. I absolutely stuffed myself on dosas. It was a great ending to a fantastic week and a half of traveling and festivities.
