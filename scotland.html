Scotland


Scotland

I've been to Scotland a few times and this last trip was a quick one to Loch Lomond, near Glasgow, for a wedding. Scottish weddings weren't something on my radar before this trip, but I know better now than to pass up an opportunity to party with the Scots. Aside from the fact that the bride and groom were two of the loveliest people I've ever met, with the nicest friends and family, there were tons of good food and booze, a band playing traditional Scottish music, and a little something called Ceilidh dancing. It's a lot wilder in person, but here is a little taste.



I definitely lost a shoe during one dance and had my feet trod on, but it was totally worth it. The wedding and reception were held in a beautiful old baronial manor house (read castle) turned youth hostel, where everyone was booked in for the weekend. The Loch Lomond Youth Hostel has since closed, which is a shame because it is a beautiful house in a lovely part of the country.

After the wedding we spent just one night in Glasgow before heading back to the states, in a trendy little hostel in the city center called The Brunswick. Our room was modern looking but cosy and the cafe downstairs opened up bright and early so we could get a bite to eat before heading to the airport.

The best surprise during our time in Glasgow though was the incredibly tasty food at Dakhin, a South Indian restaurant right around the corner. I could have eaten those dosas till the cows came home. It was a nice ending to a fantastic week and a half of traveling and festivities.
