The Sights

My trip to Portugal was a relatively short jaunt tacked on to a larger itinerary that included a night in London and a weekend in Glasgow for a friend's wedding at Loch Lomond. It was a bit of a whirlwind. My friend and I had about a day and a half in Lisbon to wander around the city and we did one major touristy thing, climb the Belem Tower. Climbing to the top of local landmarks is one of my favorite things to do in any city (Arc de Triomphe, Sagrada Familia, Sydney Harbor Bridge and hopefully many more to come), so this was right up my alley. The fort itself is beautiful and there was a nice view of the Tagus River from the top. I enjoyed the tram ride over as well because it gave us an opportunity to check out the colorful tiled facades typical of the buildings in Lisbon.

Food and Booze

The best thing we did in Lisbon was dinner at a fado restaurant. I knew nothing about fado before traveling to Portugal, so I'm so glad that my friend insisted on it. Fado is very dramatic, typically melancholy music that originated in Portugal and is usually performed by a female singer accompanied by a guitarist. I enjoyed the food as well, but we were really there for the music.

The Nitty Gritty

We stayed at a great hostel in Lisbon called Pensão Royal. Paula, the woman who runs the hostel, was a total sweetheart who cooked all kinds of beautiful cakes and pastries every morning for breakfast. My friend and I shared a private ensuite room and the bathroom was clean and modern and the decor in our room, and throughout the hostel, was really fun and colorful. I highly recommend staying there, especially if Paula is still presiding.
